The Story

From a career to a calling.

When I decided to embrace becoming a Life Sherpa, I took a leap of faith — and transitioned myself from a career to a calling.

For 27 years I was blessed to help build a family business from the ground up. We started as a company of three — my wife, my brother, and myself — and grew into a global team of more than 50. We were honored with awards of distinction, earned the loyalty of hundreds of customers, and partnered with thousands of people around the world.

It was also 27 years chock full of change, stress, and crises — the challenging by-product that comes with the territory. Over those years, we reinvented ourselves many times. I learned first-hand how difficult change and transition can be, how most of us only accept change after its benefits have been proven, and how, in the meantime, we suffer with worry, guilt, anger, resentment, and overwhelm.

I know that struggle because I lived it. I also know what pulled me through: the spark of inspiration and insight from others who had walked the path before me. The tools I learned helped me through the darkest days of my life, and being a Life Sherpa is how I now share them — guiding others to the summit of what once looked like an emotional Mount Everest, and standing there with them when they reach it.

The promise, in my own handwriting.

The Approach

Coaching as a Sherpa mountain guide.

Every meaningful climb needs a guide who knows the terrain. As your Sherpa, we walk it together.

  • Plan your climb — clarify where you want to go and what matters
  • Assess your readiness and what tools you'll need for the journey
  • Examine what you already have — strengths ready to be developed
  • Support you at every step, through every pace change
  • Allow for the elements we can't control — and flow with them

The climb moves through five stages — self-awareness, self-acceptance, self-compassion, self-care, and self-love — whatever mountain you're climbing: school, career, money, relationships, or health.

What Is EFT Tapping?

Energy psychology, grounded in science.

Although frequently overlooked, emotional health is critical to physical health and healing. No matter how devoted you are to the right diet or lifestyle, you're unlikely to achieve optimal health if emotional barriers stand in your way.

EFT uses an acupressure technique based on the same energy meridians as traditional acupuncture — used for over 5,000 years — but without the needles. Developed in the 1990s by Stanford engineer Gary Craig, it creates a cognitive shift that relieves anxiety and fear.

EFT has met the American Psychological Association's criteria for evidence-based treatments for a number of conditions, including PTSD. Clinical trials consistently show strong effect sizes after relatively few sessions.
